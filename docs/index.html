What is DeepQuest

Built different, by design

Most QA generators rely on language models to fabricate plausible-sounding questions. DeepQuest does the opposite — it mines real facts from the web, verifies them across independent sources, and constructs questions that are provably answerable.

🚫
No LLMs
Entirely deterministic. No generative AI at any stage of the pipeline. Facts are extracted by spaCy NLP, not hallucinated by a language model. The same input always produces the same output.
🔗
6+ Verified Sources
Every question requires corroboration from 6 or more independent web domains before it is accepted into the dataset. A fact that only one source reports never makes it through.
🧠
Multi-Hop Reasoning
Questions require chaining 2–3 facts across independent sources — designed to defeat single-source lookup and expose weaknesses in RAG pipelines and AI retrieval systems.

How It Works

A 6-step deterministic pipeline

Every question DeepQuest produces has passed through the same rigorous pipeline — from raw web crawl to verified, multi-hop adversarial prompt.

1
🕷️ Deep Crawling

An async HTTP crawler (httpx + trafilatura) recursively fetches pages from historical archives, newspaper digitisation projects, regulatory filings, and regional journalism sources. Raw HTML is stored in PostgreSQL for downstream processing.

crawler/worker.py
2
🔬 NLP Extraction

spaCy (en_core_web_lg) parses each page and extracts Subject-Verb-Object triples deterministically. No probabilistic generation — the same text always yields the same triples.

extractor/worker.py
3
🕸️ Knowledge Graph

Extracted triples are pushed into Neo4j. When multiple independent sources report the same relationship, the edge's domain count increases. Only edges with sufficient multi-domain support are eligible for question generation.

graph/schema.py
4
🔍 Source Enrichment

For each graph edge, the enricher actively searches Wikipedia, DBpedia, Archive.org, Open Library, Chronicling America, and Wikiwand to accumulate 6+ independent URLs confirming the same fact. Edges that cannot reach the threshold are excluded.

seeder/enrich_sources.py
5
⚠️ Contradiction Detection

The contradiction detector scans the graph for facts where sources disagree — conflicting dates, disputed attributions, or contradictory outcomes. Any disputed fact is flagged and automatically excluded from generation.

seeder/detect_contradictions.py
6
✍️ Question Generation

Rule-based templates traverse verified multi-hop graph chains and build adversarial narrative prompts. No LLM is involved. The output includes the prompt, answer, source URLs, explanation, fact fanout, and a search trajectory.

generator/query_engine.py
Example Output

What a DeepQuest question looks like

Each generated question is a structured adversarial prompt with a unique, verifiable answer backed by 6+ independent sources.

question_20260517_202141_a4c46c7e_1870.txt 
PROMPT:
In 1870, a businessman founded an oil company that would go on to control
approximately 90% of U.S. oil refining within a decade. That company was
subsequently dissolved by a landmark Supreme Court ruling in 1911.
Using at least six independent sources, identify the company.

ANSWER: STANDARD OIL

SOURCES: 6 independent domains verified
  1. en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Oil
  2. britannica.com/topic/Standard-Oil-Company-and-Trust
  3. archive.org/details/standardoilcompany
  4. chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/...
  5. dbpedia.org/resource/Standard_Oil
  6. history.com/topics/industrial-revolution/standard-oil

EXPLANATION:
The answer is Standard Oil. Multiple independent sources confirm the following
chain of facts: John D. Rockefeller → FOUNDED → Standard Oil (1870) →
CONTROLLED → ~90% U.S. oil refining → DISSOLVED BY → Supreme Court (1911).
Each cited source independently corroborates at least one link in this chain.

SEARCH_TRAJECTORY:
  1. Search for 'oil company founded 1870 controlled refining' to identify candidates.
  2. Cross-reference with historical archives (Internet Archive, Chronicling America).
  3. Verify the 1911 Supreme Court dissolution against legal databases.
  4. Confirm using at least 6 independent sources from different domains.
  5. Check that no other entity satisfies both conditions to ensure uniqueness.

Use Cases

What you can do with DeepQuest

DeepQuest produces datasets purpose-built to stress-test AI systems in ways that synthetic or LLM-generated benchmarks simply cannot.

01
RAG Benchmark Testing
Test retrieval-augmented generation systems against questions that require multi-source reasoning. Single-document retrieval will fail — by design. Expose gaps in your pipeline before they reach production.
02
AI Hallucination Research
Questions are engineered to expose when AI systems fabricate answers. Because every correct answer is uniquely verifiable, any deviation is a measurable hallucination — not an ambiguous edge case.
03
Historical QA Datasets
Build high-quality, verifiable datasets for academic research. Export to HuggingFace JSONL, SQuAD v2, HotpotQA, JSON, or CSV — ready to drop into your training or evaluation pipeline.
